    • Environmental Services

    IDC has performed three recent projects in the Port of Miami. In January of 2011 at the Pilot Station Shoreline IDC performed the required relocation of 40 octocoral and stony coral colonies. Next, in June of 2011 on the southeast of the Miami Beach Marina near the Government Cut Channel IDC performed the required relocation of 36 octocoral and stony coral colonies. Then, from October 2011 to February 2012 in the Port of Miami Container Crane Wharves I-VI, IDC surveyed to locate and identify coral colonies found along the 6,000 linear foot seawall and then removed and transplanted 180 coral colonies. Some of the stony coral colonies located on Wharf I were in excess of 30 years old and weighed over 100 lbs. All three of the project listed here will require IDC to perform 6 month monitoring events for two years.
